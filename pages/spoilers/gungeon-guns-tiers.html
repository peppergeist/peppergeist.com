The Guns of Enter The Gungeon: A Definitive Tier List

I have painstakingly ranked every single gun of this game into tiers based on usefulness.

But why would you do such a thing

I'll tell you why. Enter The Gungeon defines its own tiers of rarity for all of the guns, but rarity more often than not doesn't correlate with actual usefulness of the gun. Some D-tier guns are excellent, and some S-tier guns are near worthless. Since rarity is only a rough indicator of gun efficacy and you don't want to waste your time slogging through the game with the wrong gun, I have defined my own tiers as followed:
Useless
This gun should never be used unless circumstances are particularly dire. Even your starting weapon would be better than this gun.
Okay
This gun should be a backup and is not very useful. However, it will work if nothing better is available. This gun is comparable to starting weaponry.
Good
This gun can be used for most situations and should not be discarded if it can be avoided.
Great
This gun can be relied on for the entire game.
Bonkers
This gun makes the game so easy it might as well be playing itself.
Also, I might add (room) or (boss) after the ranking to indicate that the gun is much more useful when clearing rooms or fighting bosses, respectively.
