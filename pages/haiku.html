Haiku

Sometimes, I write haiku. On this page, they are arranged by year, and then by topic. A word of warning: the vast majority of all poetry is bad and not worth reading. These poems are no exception.

2018

"One Year"

Ready for summer
I sit behind the window
Glaring at the sleet
Ready for winter
I sit underneath the shade
Squinting at the sun
The red leaves of fall
Trees will not show life again
Until next spring comes
The new blooms of spring
Life indefatigable
At least until next fall

"The Workday"

Waiting for the bus
I think of things I must do
My mind is spinning
While I am at work
My hands labor for their wage
My mind is elsewhere
On the bus to home
I regret how little time
The evening has left
While I am at home
My mind only thinks of work
My hands idle, I sigh

"The Couch"

Sleeping on the couch
Like a guest in my own home
What lousy service
This couch is comfy
Why does my couch feel better
Than the bed I built?
Because I built it?
Could the mattress be too firm?
Or is it too soft?
Maybe small changes
Are comforting, even if but
Sleeping on the couch

"Tori"

Murder of loud crows
Outside my window at night
Want to murder them
A flock of seagulls
Why are they here at the park?
Oh, there's hot dog buns
A tiny sparrow
Hopping about in a bush
Not very birdlike
Man with a parrot
It walks in front, so more like
Parrot with a man

"Seattle"

The Emerald City
The buildings aren't really green
There's just lots of trees
Hippie restaurants
There's at least three on each block
None of them worth it
The air is so fresh
Even when I'm in downtown
Allergies abound
The locals are odd
I do not want to approach
Nude man smeared in poop

"Fall Morning"

This crisp morning air
Which hangs about my tired face
Remains unvalued
I shut the windows
Locking out the morning breeze
In lieu of coffee
Now warmed up, I go
From my realm to one of cold
Ready to be chilled
The sting on my face
Better than any caffeine
I drink it all in
